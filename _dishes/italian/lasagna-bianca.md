## Before the Tomato

Lasagna Bianca isn't a modern invention—it's actually the **original** Italian lasagna. Before tomatoes arrived from the New World in the 16th century, all Italian lasagna was "bianca"—layered with soft cheese, spices, and meat, bound together with rich béchamel.

This elegant, creamy style remains popular throughout Italy, particularly in regions like Emilia-Romagna and Umbria.

## Regional Variations

- **Emilia-Romagna**: White ragù with walnuts
- **Venice**: Extra butter, sometimes walnuts
- **Puglia**: Burrata and cardoncelli mushrooms
- **Umbria**: Pure béchamel with sage-infused butter, no tomato in sight

## Ingredients

### Béchamel Sauce (Rich Version)
- 1 liter whole milk
- 100g butter
- 100g flour
- Generous freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and white pepper

### Cheese Mixture
- 500g ricotta
- 200g mozzarella, shredded
- 150g fontina, cubed
- 150g Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
- 2 large eggs
- Salt and pepper

### Mushroom-Spinach Filling (Optional)
- 500g mixed mushrooms, sliced
- 300g fresh spinach
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp butter
- Fresh thyme
- Salt and pepper

### OR Chicken Filling
- 500g cooked chicken, shredded
- 200g mushrooms, sliced
- Fresh sage and thyme

### Pasta & Assembly
- 12-15 lasagna sheets (fresh or no-boil)
- Extra Parmigiano for topping
- Fresh parsley or sage for garnish

## Instructions

### Make the Béchamel

1. **Melt butter** over medium heat in a heavy saucepan.

2. **Add flour**: Whisk in flour, cook 2-3 minutes until it smells nutty but hasn't browned.

3. **Add milk gradually**: Pour warm milk in a slow stream, whisking constantly.

4. **Cook**: Continue whisking until thick enough to coat a spoon (10-12 minutes).

5. **Season**: Add nutmeg generously, plus salt and white pepper.

### Prepare the Mushroom-Spinach Filling

1. **Sauté mushrooms**: Melt butter in a large pan over high heat. Add mushrooms, don't crowd. Cook until golden and all moisture evaporates.

2. **Add garlic**: Stir in garlic and thyme, cook 1 minute.

3. **Add spinach**: Add spinach in batches, wilting each addition. Season.

4. **Cool**: Let mixture cool before assembling.

### Make the Cheese Mixture

1. **Combine**: Mix ricotta, mozzarella, fontina, half the Parmigiano, and eggs.

2. **Season**: Add salt and pepper.

### Assemble the Lasagna

1. **Preheat oven** to 350°F (180°C).

2. **Start with béchamel**: Spread thin layer on bottom of 9x13 inch dish.

3. **First pasta layer**: Lay pasta sheets in single layer.

4. **Build**:
   - Spread cheese mixture
   - Add mushroom-spinach filling (if using)
   - Drizzle béchamel
   - Sprinkle Parmigiano

5. **Repeat**: Continue layering (4-5 pasta layers total).

6. **Top layer**: End with pasta, béchamel, and generous Parmigiano.

7. **Bake covered**: 350°F for 30 minutes.

8. **Uncover and brown**: Increase to 400°F (200°C), bake 15-20 minutes until golden and bubbling.

9. **Rest**: Let stand 10-15 minutes before cutting.

## The Two-Temperature Technique

Starting at 350°F ensures the center cooks through without burning the top. Increasing to 400°F at the end creates that beautiful golden crust.

## Cheese Selection Guide

| Cheese | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Ricotta** | Creaminess, moisture |
| **Mozzarella** | Stretch, melting |
| **Fontina** | Nuttiness, smoothness |
| **Parmigiano** | Sharp, umami, browning |
| **Gruyère** | Optional substitute for fontina |

## Variations

### Chicken White Lasagna
Replace mushroom-spinach filling with:
- Shredded rotisserie chicken
- Sautéed shallots
- Fresh sage
- Splash of white wine

### Seafood White Lasagna
- Sautéed shrimp and crab
- Lemon zest in béchamel
- Fresh dill

### Truffle White Lasagna
- Add truffle oil to béchamel
- Shaved truffle between layers
- Pure luxury

## Tips for Success

1. **Season every layer**: Lasagna can be bland without enough salt
2. **Use warm milk**: Prevents lumps in béchamel
3. **Don't skimp on nutmeg**: It's the soul of white lasagna
4. **Let it rest**: Structure sets during resting

## Make-Ahead

- **Béchamel**: Make 2 days ahead, press plastic wrap directly on surface
- **Fillings**: Prepare day before
- **Assembled**: Refrigerate unbaked up to 24 hours
- **Freeze**: Up to 3 months unbaked
